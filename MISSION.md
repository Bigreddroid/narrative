# The Narrative — Mission

**We tell you how the world's next shock will hit your life — before it does, with the evidence.**

## The real-life problem

Ordinary people live downstream of events they never see coming. A conflict on another continent, a policy change, a shipping disruption — weeks later it shows up as a higher cooking-gas bill, a job at risk, a food-price spike, a loan that got more expensive. People get **headlines**, not **"what this means for me."** They are blindsided, every day, by consequences that were predictable.

## What we do

The Narrative reads the world's news across many sources, separates what's agreed from what's disputed, and maps the **causal chain** from a global event down to its effect on *your* daily life — with an exact piece of evidence and a fact/inference/speculation label at every step, plus a timeline ("~6 weeks") and a severity. Not "prices may rise." Instead: *"cooking-gas likely up 12–18% within 6 weeks based on reduced tanker throughput — here's the source."*

## Who we serve first — India

1.4 billion people, mobile-first, acutely exposed to global price shocks in fuel, food, and fertiliser, and underserved by Western finance tools. The engine is already built around Indian citizen-level consequences. This is where the mission is most needed and traction is fastest. Then we expand outward.

## Principles (non-negotiable)

- **Evidence at every node.** Every claim carries its source sentence.
- **Label honesty.** VERIFIED FACT vs INFERRED MECHANISM vs SPECULATIVE EFFECT — never blur them.
- **Never blindside, never hype.** Specific, calm, useful. No fear-farming.
- **Keep score.** We log our predictions and whether they came true. Public track record, not vibes.

## How it stays free

Free for citizens. **Enterprises — funds, risk desks, insurers, government — pay for the exact same foresight, and their fees keep the consumer side free.** Same engine, two audiences. See `PITCH.md`.

## Not a better Bloomberg — a different one

The powerful pay ~$30,000 a seat for a terminal that tells them what's happening in markets. We're not trying to out-feature that. We answer a different question — *"what happens next, to you"* — for the people Bloomberg was never built for, and give it away. The contrast is the point: **the foresight of the few, handed to everyone.**
